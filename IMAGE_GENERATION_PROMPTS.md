## 1. GIOTTO_MAESTA
**Filename:** `GIOTTO_MAESTA.jpg`
**Artist:** Giotto di Bondone | **Year:** c. 1310 | **Room:** A4

```
Ognissanti Madonna (Madonna Enthroned) by Giotto, circa 1310. Monumental altarpiece showing the Virgin Mary seated on an elaborate Gothic marble throne holding the Christ Child, surrounded by angels and saints. Revolutionary break from Byzantine rigidity: figures have real weight and volume, natural drapery folds, human expressions with subtle smiles. Gold leaf background with delicate halos. Three-dimensional architectural throne with convincing perspective depth. Tempera on wood panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 2. BOTTICELLI_PRIMAVERA
**Filename:** `BOTTICELLI_PRIMAVERA.jpg`
**Artist:** Sandro Botticelli | **Year:** c. 1482 | **Room:** A9

```
Primavera (Spring) by Sandro Botticelli, circa 1480. Large mythological painting in a dark orange grove. Venus stands at center in red dress with Cupid above. The Three Graces dance in transparent white robes on the left, Mercury reaches up with his caduceus. On the right, blue Zephyr pursues the nymph Chloris who transforms into Flora in a flower-patterned dress scattering roses. Over 500 botanical species painted. Dark forest background. Tempera on panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery.
```

## 3. BOTTICELLI_BIRTH_OF_VENUS
**Filename:** `BOTTICELLI_BIRTH_OF_VENUS.jpg`
**Artist:** Sandro Botticelli | **Year:** c. 1484 | **Room:** A9

```
The Birth of Venus by Sandro Botticelli, 1485. The nude goddess Venus emerges from the sea as a fully grown woman, standing on a giant scallop shell. Zephyr, the west wind, blows her toward shore while the nymph Pomona waits with a flowing red cloak decorated with flowers. Venus has long flowing golden hair covering her body modestly. Pale luminous skin, turquoise Aegean sea, roses blown in the wind. Tempera on canvas. The most iconic Renaissance painting. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 4. FILIPPO_LIPPI_MADONNA
**Filename:** `FILIPPO_LIPPI_MADONNA.jpg`
**Artist:** Filippo Lippi | **Year:** c. 1465 | **Room:** A9

```
Madonna and Child with Two Angels by Filippo Lippi, circa 1465. The Virgin Mary sits elegantly before an open window overlooking an elaborate rocky landscape. She wears a transparent veil over her hair, gazing down with lowered eyes. Two young angels support the Christ Child, one looking out at the viewer with a mischievous smile. The Madonna modeled on Lucrezia Buti, the nun Lippi fell in love with. Soft modeling, pearl-like complexion. Tempera on panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 5. TRIBUNA_MEDICI_VENUS
**Filename:** `TRIBUNA_MEDICI_VENUS.jpg`
**Artist:** Unknown (Roman copy) | **Year:** 1st century BC | **Room:** A16

```
Medici Venus, ancient Roman marble sculpture copy of a Greek original, 1st century BC. Life-size nude female figure in modest Venus Pudica pose, covering herself with her hands. Smooth white marble, idealized female beauty, contrapposto stance. Displayed in the famous octagonal Tribuna room since 1688. One of the most celebrated ancient sculptures in the world. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 6. DURER_ADORATION
**Filename:** `DURER_ADORATION.jpg`
**Artist:** Albrecht Durer | **Year:** 1504 | **Room:** A20

```
Adoration of the Magi by Albrecht Durer, 1504. German Renaissance masterpiece. The Three Magi present gifts to the infant Christ held by the Virgin Mary under architectural ruins. Durer depicted himself as the second king with golden hair and beard. Richly dressed kings with exotic turbans and elaborate robes. Detailed Northern Renaissance style: every brick, fabric fold, and facial hair rendered precisely. Ruined classical arch symbolizing the passing of the old world. Oil on wood panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 7. PIERO_DELLA_FRANCESCA_DIPTYCH
**Filename:** `PIERO_DELLA_FRANCESCA_DIPTYCH.jpg`
**Artist:** Piero della Francesca | **Year:** c. 1465-1472 | **Room:** A25

```
Portraits of Federico da Montefeltro and Battista Sforza (Diptych) by Piero della Francesca, circa 1472. Double portrait in ancient Roman coin profile style. The Duke has a distinctive hooked broken nose, ruddy complexion, wearing a red cap. The Duchess has pale porcelain skin, elaborate blonde hairstyle with pearls, jeweled necklace against black dress. Behind both figures stretches an expansive Umbrian landscape with aerial perspective: winding rivers, rolling green hills, distant towns, hazy blue mountains. Tempera on panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 8. LEONARDO_ANNUNCIATION
**Filename:** `LEONARDO_ANNUNCIATION.jpg`
**Artist:** Leonardo da Vinci | **Year:** c. 1472 | **Room:** A35

```
The Annunciation by Leonardo da Vinci, circa 1472-1475. The Archangel Gabriel kneels in a lush Renaissance garden with detailed wings based on a bird of prey, holding a white Madonna lily. The Virgin Mary sits at an ornate marble lectern with an open book, dressed in blue and red robes. Behind them, a walled garden with cypress trees and a distant harbor with ships, misty mountains using atmospheric perspective. Early sfumato technique. Oil and tempera on panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 9. MICHELANGELO_TONDO_DONI
**Filename:** `MICHELANGELO_TONDO_DONI.jpg`
**Artist:** Michelangelo Buonarroti | **Year:** c. 1506-1508 | **Room:** A38

```
Doni Tondo (The Holy Family) by Michelangelo, 1507. Circular painting (tondo format) in an original ornate gilded frame carved by Michelangelo. The Madonna in brilliant pink and blue robes twists to receive the Christ Child from Saint Joseph behind her. Powerful sculptural muscular figures with complex spiraling poses. Vibrant cangiante colors: orange, blue, green, pink with iridescent effects. Nude male figures visible in the background behind a wall. Tempera on panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.

## 10. RAPHAEL_MADONNA_GOLDFINCH
**Filename:** `RAPHAEL_MADONNA_GOLDFINCH.jpg`
**Artist:** Raphael | **Year:** c. 1505-1506 | **Room:** A38

```
Madonna of the Goldfinch by Raphael, 1506. Serene High Renaissance pyramidal composition in a Tuscan landscape. The Virgin Mary in red dress and blue mantle sits reading a book, the infant Christ Child stands between her knees gently touching a goldfinch held by the young John the Baptist in animal skins. The goldfinch symbolizes Christ's future Passion. Soft atmospheric landscape with rolling hills, distant town, and hazy blue sky. Perfect classical balance and tender expressions. Oil on panel. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.

## 11. TITIAN_VENUS_URBINO
**Filename:** `TITIAN_VENUS_URBINO.jpg`
**Artist:** Titian | **Year:** 1538 | **Room:** D23

```
Venus of Urbino by Titian, 1538. A sensual young nude woman reclines on a bed with rumpled white sheets and a red-brown coverlet, looking directly at the viewer with a confident inviting gaze. ## 12. CARAVAGGIO_MEDUSA
**Filename:** `CARAVAGGIO_MEDUSA.jpg`
**Artist:** Caravaggio | **Year:** c. 1595-1598 | **Room:** E4

```
Medusa by Caravaggio, 1597. Oil painted on a convex wooden ceremonial shield. The severed head of the Gorgon Medusa at the exact moment of decapitation, her face contorted in a scream of horror and disbelief. Bulging eyes, open mouth showing teeth, writhing green snakes for hair. Blood spurts from the severed neck. Believed to be Caravaggio's self-portrait. Dramatic spotlight against dark background. Commissioned for Grand Duke Ferdinando I de' Medici. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery.
```

## 13. ARTEMISIA_JUDITH
**Filename:** `ARTEMISIA_JUDITH.jpg`
**Artist:** Artemisia Gentileschi | **Year:** c. 1620 | **Room:** E4

```
Judith Beheading Holofernes by Artemisia Gentileschi, circa 1620. Violent and powerful Baroque painting showing Judith and her maidservant in the act of decapitating the Assyrian general Holofernes on a blood-soaked bed. Judith's sleeves are rolled up, her strong arms pushing the sword through his neck, blood spurting onto white sheets. Determined female faces, muscular effort. Rich fabrics: gold dress with blue sleeves. Dramatic Caravaggesque tenebrism with stark diagonal lighting. Oil on canvas. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
```

## 14. CERUTI_MENDICANTE
**Filename:** `CERUTI_MENDICANTE.jpg`
**Artist:** Giacomo Ceruti | **Year:** c. 1730 | **Room:** D18

```
Il Mendicante Moro (The Moorish Beggar) by Giacomo Ceruti, circa 1730. A portrait of a beggar depicted with the regal dignity of a king, challenging 18th-century social norms. The subject gazes directly at the viewer with quiet dignity despite humble circumstances. Realistic portrayal of poverty without sentimentality. Earth tones and muted palette typical of Ceruti's pitocchi (beggar) paintings. Acquired by the Uffizi in November 2025. Oil on canvas. Museum photograph, Uffizi Gallery Florence.
